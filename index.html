About

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), car accidents are one of the leading causes of death in the U.S., causing around thirty-five thousand of deaths per year.

To understand the cause of car accidents, it is important to understand both demographic information about drivers, and external factors such as weather, that may contribute to accidents.

This project aims to visually show demographic information of people involved in car accidents in Chicago, such as age and sex.

In addition, this project will visually depict other external factors such as weather, day of the week and time of day.

With this information, policymakers would have a clear way to visualize the characteristics of accidents and then the use the information gleaned from these visualizations to drive public policy. For example, if the visualizations show a marked increase in accidents during particular types of weather, policymakers could undertake educational campaigns to warn drivers about the risks in driving in such weather.
